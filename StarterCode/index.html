Correlations Discovered Between the Availability of Healthcare and Poverty

Looking at the above charts, we can draw the following insights:

  • States with poverty rates below 13%, we do not see much Correlation between levels of povery and lack of healthcare. For example, NH has about 11.4% lacking healthcare for 9% poverty rate. On the other hand, MA has about 4.6 lacking healthcare for 11.6% povery rate
  • For states with poverty rates above 13%, we see a more apparent Correlation between levels of poverty and lack of healthcare. As povery levels increase, the percent of people lacking healthcare increases as well.
